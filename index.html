About Periodic Trends

Atomic Radius: Generally decreases across a period (left to right) and increases down a group.

Ionization Energy: Generally increases across a period and decreases down a group.

Electronegativity: Generally increases across a period and decreases down a group. Fluorine is most electronegative.

Common Chemical Hazards

Chemical H-Statements P-Statements NFPA

NFPA Diamond Ratings

  • Health (Blue): 0 = Normal material, 1 = Slightly hazardous, 2 = Hazardous, 3 = Extreme danger, 4 = Deadly
  • Flammability (Red): 0 = Will not burn, 1 = Must be preheated, 2 = Ignites when heated, 3 = Ignites at room temp, 4 = Burns readily
  • Reactivity (Yellow): 0 = Stable, 1 = Unstable if heated, 2 = Violent reaction, 3 = Shock/heat may detonate, 4 = May detonate
  • Special (White): W = Water reactive, OX = Oxidizer, COR = Corrosive, ACID = Acid, ALK = Alkali, BIO = Biohazard, RAD = Radioactive
